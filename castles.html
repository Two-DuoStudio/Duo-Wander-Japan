Japan Castles

Samurai Legends Still Guard Timeless Walls

Himeji Castle

Hyogo

Himeji Castle: White Heron of Japan

✦ Located in Hyōgo Prefecture, overlooking the city of Himeji
✦ Nicknamed the “White Heron Castle” for its elegant, wing-like silhouette
✦ Completed in the early 17th century during the Edo period
✦ Known for brilliant white plaster walls that catch the light
✦ Considered the finest surviving example of feudal Japanese castle design
✦ Complex includes over eighty buildings, gates, and defensive paths
✦ Features a five-story main keep rising above layered roofs
✦ Surrounded by moats, stone walls, and seasonal cherry trees
✦ Designed with clever defensive layouts and hidden passageways
✦ Survived wars, earthquakes, and extensive restorations over centuries
✦ Popular in spring for cherry-blossom viewing around the grounds
✦ Himeji Castle embodies history, resilience, and classical beauty

Matsumoto Castle

Nagano

Matsumoto Castle: Crow of the Alps

✦ Located in Nagano Prefecture, surrounded by the Japanese Alps
✦ Known as the “Crow Castle” for its striking black exterior
✦ Construction began in the late 16th century during the Sengoku period
✦ Features a six-story main keep rising over a wide moat
✦ One of the oldest surviving castles still in its original wooden form
✦ Interior has steep stairs, wooden floors, and warrior displays
✦ Windows were designed for archers and gunners in defense
✦ Reflects beautifully in the moat, especially on calm days
✦ Surrounded by cherry trees, creating stunning spring scenery
✦ Offers panoramic views of the Northern Alps from its tower
✦ Hosts seasonal festivals, night illuminations, and cultural events
✦ Matsumoto Castle symbolizes strength, elegance, and living history

Osaka Castle

Osaka

Osaka Castle: Pride of the Samurai City

✦ Originally built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi
✦ Symbolized the unification of Japan during his reign
✦ Famous for its massive stone walls and golden decorations
✦ Burned down during the Siege of Osaka in 1615
✦ Rebuilt in the 1620s under Tokugawa rule
✦ Main tower reconstructed in 1931 with modern techniques
✦ Surrounded by wide moats and lush parkland
✦ Features ornate rooftop ornaments shaped like shachi (mythical fish)
✦ Castle museum showcases samurai armor and historical artifacts
✦ Popular cherry blossom viewing spot in spring
✦ Heavily restored after World War II bombings
✦ A vibrant symbol of Osaka’s history and pride

Nagoya Castle

Aichi

Nagoya Castle: The Golden Fortress

✦ Built in 1612 under the Tokugawa shogunate as a strategic stronghold
✦ Famous for its dazzling golden shachihoko (mythical tiger-fish) roof ornaments
✦ Designed as the political and military center of the Owari Tokugawa branch
✦ Massive stone walls and deep moats show advanced defensive engineering
✦ One of Japan’s largest castles, symbolizing Tokugawa power and prestige
✦ The main keep (tenshu) originally rose about 48 meters
✦ Served as an administrative and cultural hub for the region for centuries
✦ Heavily damaged by bombing in 1945 during World War II
✦ Reconstructed main keep completed in 1959 using reinforced concrete
✦ The interior displays samurai armor, weapons, and historical exhibits
✦ Current projects aim to restore the keep in traditional wooden form
✦ Castle grounds bloom with cherry blossoms, drawing crowds each spring

Matsue Castle

Shimane

Matsue Castle: The Black Fortress

✦ Completed in 1611 by Horio Yoshiharu, a powerful feudal lord
✦ Nicknamed the “Black Castle” for its dark wooden exterior
✦ One of only 12 original castles in Japan still standing today
✦ Features a unique “watchtower” style main keep (tenshu)
✦ Built with steep stone walls and moats for strong defense
✦ Interior designed with hidden spaces for warriors and supplies
✦ Provides sweeping views of Lake Shinji and surrounding city
✦ A rare example of authentic early Edo period architecture
✦ Houses historical artifacts, samurai armor, and weapons
✦ Declared a National Treasure of Japan in 2015
✦ Popular for spring cherry blossom viewing in the castle park
✦ Strong symbol of Matsue’s samurai heritage and pride

Kumamoto Castle

Kumamoto

Kumamoto Castle: Fortress of Strength and Resilience

✦ Built in 1607 by warlord Katō Kiyomasa
✦ Renowned for its imposing black-and-white exterior
✦ Famous for ingenious defensive designs and steep stone walls
✦ Known as “Ginkgo Castle” for its iconic ginkgo trees
✦ Played a major role in the Satsuma Rebellion of 1877
✦ Large complex once had over 80 buildings
✦ Main keep reconstructed in the 1960s after wartime destruction
✦ Extensive grounds include turrets, gates, and moats
✦ Earthquake in 2016 caused heavy damage to structures
✦ Ongoing restoration symbolizes strength and perseverance
✦ Popular site for samurai history enthusiasts
✦ A proud landmark of Kumamoto’s cultural heritage
