Mount Fuji

Honshu

Mount Fuji: Japan's Sacred Peak

✦ Japan's tallest mountain, located on the border of Shizuoka and Yamanashi
✦ An active stratovolcano, last erupted in 1707-1708
✦ Considered a sacred symbol in Shinto and Buddhist traditions
✦ A UNESCO World Heritage Site for its cultural and spiritual significance
✦ Famous for its perfect symmetrical cone shape
✦ Popular for climbing, with official season from early July to early September
✦ Offers stunning sunrise views from the summit, known as Goraiko
✦ Surrounding Fuji Five Lakes region offers hiking, boating, and scenic views
✦ Featured in many artworks, including Hokusai's “Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji”
✦ Winter snow-capped peak is a photographer's favorite
✦ The mountain is visible from Tokyo on clear days, inspiring awe nationwide
✦ Pilgrimages to the summit have been made for centuries
✦ Mount Fuji represents beauty, endurance, and Japan's cultural identity

Mount Kita

Honshu, Akaishi Range

Mount Kita: Southern Alps Giant

✦ Second tallest mountain in Japan, located in Yamanashi Prefecture
✦ Part of the Southern Alps (Akaishi Mountains), known for rugged peaks
✦ Popular among experienced hikers and climbers for its challenging trails
✦ Offers panoramic views of Mount Fuji and surrounding valleys
✦ Flora includes alpine plants and rare wildflowers in the summer
✦ Wildlife such as Japanese serow and marmots can be spotted along trails
✦ Climbing season is typically July to September, when snow has melted
✦ Mountain trails connect to nearby peaks for extended alpine routes
✦ Known for sunrise and sunset views, creating breathtaking photo opportunities
✦ Nearby Minami Alps National Park protects natural scenery and biodiversity
✦ Historically revered in local culture as a symbol of endurance and strength
✦ Huts and shelters along trails support climbers for overnight stays
✦ Mount Kita is celebrated for its natural beauty, adventure, and alpine challenge

Mount Hotaka

Honshu, Northern Alps

Mount Hotaka: Northern Alps Majesty

✦ Located in Nagano and Gifu Prefectures, part of the Northern Japan Alps
✦ Highest peak in the Northern Alps at 3,190 meters, third tallest in Japan
✦ Popular among hikers and climbers for its challenging alpine routes
✦ Offers stunning views of surrounding peaks, valleys, and mountain lakes
✦ Home to diverse alpine flora and fauna, including rare wildflowers
✦ Climbing season runs July to September, when trails are snow-free
✦ Mount Hotaka is part of the Chūbu-Sangaku National Park
✦ Several huts and lodges along trails provide shelter for climbers
✦ Famous for breathtaking sunrise and sunset vistas
✦ Trails include ridges, steep slopes, and rocky paths for adventurous climbers
✦ Historically, the mountain holds spiritual significance in local mountain worship
✦ Nearby Kamikōchi Valley serves as a scenic gateway for treks
✦ Mount Hotaka is celebrated for its majestic peaks beauty, and alpine adventure

Mount Tate

Toyama, Northern Alps

Mount Tate: Northern Alps Sacred Peak

✦ Located in Toyama Prefecture, part of the Northern Japan Alps
✦ One of Japan's Three Holy Mountains, along with Mount Fuji and Mount Haku
✦ Reaches a height of 3,015 meters, known for its rugged beauty
✦ Famous for Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route, offering scenic mountain travel
✦ Home to hot springs, alpine flora, and snowy landscapes
✦ Sacred in Shinto and Buddhist traditions, attracting pilgrims for centuries
✦ Known for Gorgeous snow corridors in spring along the Alpine Route
✦ Offers hiking trails suitable for experienced climbers and trekkers
✦ Summers provide clear views of neighboring peaks and valleys
✦ Wildlife includes Japanese serow, ptarmigans, and alpine birds
✦ Winter snow creates dramatic scenery, though climbing is more challenging
✦ Mountain shrines honor local deities and spiritual traditions
✦ Mount Tate is celebrated for its natural beauty, and alpine adventure

Mount Haku

Honshu, Gifu

Mount Haku: Sacred White Mountain

✦ Located on the border of Gifu and Fukui Prefectures
✦ One of Japan's Three Holy Mountains, along with Mount Fuji and Mount Tate
✦ Reaches a height of 2,702 meters, covered in snow for much of the year
✦ Known for its spiritual significance in Shinto and Buddhist traditions
✦ Pilgrims climb to shrines at the summit to honor mountain deities
✦ Popular for hiking, especially in summer when trails are accessible
✦ Features alpine flora, including rare plants and highland flowers
✦ Offers panoramic views of surrounding peaks, valleys, and plains
✦ Winter snow creates dramatic, picturesque landscapes
✦ Nearby Hakusan National Park protects the natural environment and wildlife
✦ Wildlife includes Japanese macaques, serow, and alpine birds
✦ Traditional festivals and rituals celebrate the mountain's spiritual power
✦ Mount Haku is celebrated for its beauty, sacred heritage, and natural grandeur

Mount Daisen

Honshu, Tottori

Mount Daisen: Western Japan's Sacred

✦ Located in Tottori Prefecture, part of the Chūgoku mountain range
✦ Highest peak in the Chūgoku region at 1,729 meters
✦ Known for its forests, hiking trails, and natural beauty
✦ Considered sacred in Shinto and Buddhist traditions for centuries
✦ Home to Daisen-ji Temple, a historic mountain temple complex
✦ Popular for hiking, trekking, and nature photography
✦ Trails are lined with ancient cedar trees and alpine flora
✦ Winter brings snow, attracting skiers and winter hikers
✦ Offers panoramic views of the Sea of Japan and surrounding mountains
✦ Wildlife includes Japanese macaques, deer, and various bird species
✦ Seasonal festivals celebrate the mountain's spiritual and natural significance
✦ The mountain is a hub for meditation, pilgrimage, and cultural practices
✦ Mount Daisen is celebrated for its sacred heritage beauty, and outdoor adventure
