Kiyomizu-dera

Kyoto

Kiyomizu-dera: Pure Water Sanctuary

✦ Founded in 778 AD, it is Kyoto's oldest Buddhist temples, over 1,200 years old
✦ Located in Higashiyama district with panoramic views of Kyoto
✦ The wooden main hall, built without nails, rises on tall stilts
✦ The temple's design fits perfectly with the hillside and surrounding forests
✦ The wide veranda offers amazing views of Kyoto and its seasonal scenery
✦ Otowa Waterfall flows within the temple grounds, with three streams
✦ The three streams believed to grant longevity, success, and love
✦ Visitors can drink from the waterfall streams using special cups for blessings
✦ The temple's design fits perfectly with the hillside and surrounding forests
✦ Discover small shrines and statues scattered around the temple grounds
✦ Seiryu-e Festival in March & September celebrates Kiyomizu-dera's Blue Dragon
✦ Visit during spring for cherry blossoms or autumn for colorful red leaves
✦ Walk around the nearby streets of Sannenzaka and Ninenzaka to explore

Daigo-ji

Kyoto

Daigo-ji: Temple of the Finest Essence

✦ Founded in 874, Daigo-ji is a Buddhist temple over 1,100 years old
✦ Located in southeastern Kyoto, spreading across Mount Kamidaigo
✦ Daigo-ji Temple features a main hall, pagoda, garden, tea house, and museum
✦ The main hall holds a seated statue of Yakushi Buddha
✦ Sanbō-in, a sub-temple in the lower area, is famous for its Japanese garden
✦ Ugetsujaya tea house serves traditional tea with panoramic garden views
✦ Reihōkan Museum displays Buddhist statues, scrolls, and cultural treasures
✦ Bentendō Hall, one of the most photographed spots, sits next to a pond
✦ A one-hour hike from Bentendō Hall leads to Kami-Daigo's halls and Osaka views
✦ Daigo-ji is best in spring for cherry blossoms and autumn for maple leaves
✦ Godai-Rikison Ninno-e on Feb 23 has monks praying for peace and happiness
✦ Visitors can sip holy water from Daigo-ji's spring, said to bring good luck
✦ They can also stroll the gardens and enjoy quiet spots for photos and relaxation

Byodo-in

Kyoto

Byodo-in: Temple of Equal Peace

✦ Founded in 1052, Byodo-in is a Buddhist temple over 970 years old
✦ Located in Uji, Kyoto Prefecture, near the Uji River
✦ The temple complex includes the Phoenix Hall, gardens, museum, and tea house
✦ The Phoenix Hall houses a statue of Amida Buddha, a National Treasure
✦ The Phoenix Hall is the building featured on Japan's 10 yen coin
✦ The temple's garden is a Jōdo-style garden, designated as a National Historic Site
✦ Hoshokan Museum displays Buddhist artifacts and treasures from the temple
✦ Byodō-in Tea Salon serves Uji matcha and sweets in a relaxing atmosphere
✦ Byodo-in is best visited in autumn for fiery red maple leaves
✦ Nighttime Autumn Illumination lights up Phoenix Hall & gardens in November
✦ Visitors can stroll the temple grounds in about 40 minutes to 1 hour
✦ Visitors can explore the nearby Uji River area with open fields
✦ Boat rides on the nearby Uji River offer scenic temple views from the water

Seiganto-ji

Wakayama

Seiganto-ji: Temple of the Blue Shore

✦ Founded in the 4th century, a Tendai Buddhist temple over 1,600 years old
✦ Located in Nachikatsuura, Wakayama Prefecture, at the base of Mount Nachi
✦ The temple has Main Hall, Three-Story Pagoda, treasure hall, and lantern halls
✦ The temple is beside Nachi Falls, Japan's tallest waterfall at 133 meters.
✦ The pagoda with the waterfall backdrop is a famous photo spot
✦ Visitors climb stone steps lined with ancient cedar trees
✦ Pilgrims stop here on the sacred Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes
✦ The temple is dedicated to Kannon, the Goddess of Mercy
✦ Annual fire festival Nachi-no-Hi Matsuri is held in July
✦ The bright vermilion pagoda contrasts beautifully with green forests
✦ Nearby attractions include Kumano Nachi Taisha Grand Shrine
✦ Easily reached by bus from Kii-Katsuura Station on the JR line

Kongobu-ji

Wakayama

Kongobu-ji: Diamond Peak Temple

✦ Founded in 1593, the temple was built to honor his late mother
✦ Kongobu-ji serves as the head temple of Shingon Buddhism on Mount Koya
✦ It is located in the sacred town of Koyasan in Wakayama Prefecture
✦ The main hall showcases traditional Japanese wooden architecture
✦ The Banryutei Garden, Japan's largest rock garden, covers 2,340 square meters
✦ The garden design represents two dragons emerging from a sea of clouds
✦ Inside, visitors find sliding doors painted with delicate 16th-century artworks
✦ The Willow Room marks the site where Hidetsugu performed ritual suicide
✦ Large tatami rooms once hosted important tea ceremonies and temple meetings
✦ The temple grounds are especially beautiful in cherry blossoms and autumn
✦ Visitors can explore nearby sites such as Danjo Garan and Okunoin Cemetery
✦ The calm temple gardens and sacred halls offer a peaceful retreat for travelers

Ruriko-in

Kyoto

Ruriko-in: Temple of Seasonal Beauty

✦ Rurikō-in was founded in the early Edo period as a small Buddhist temple
✦ It is located in the Yase area at the foot of Mount Hiei in Kyoto
✦ The temple is famous for its stunning seasonal foliage and serene atmosphere
✦ Visitors can admire autumn maple leaves reflected on the polished wooden floors
✦ In spring, the temple offers views of fresh greenery and tranquil gardens
✦ The main hall features traditional wooden architecture and tatami floors
✦ The gardens are designed to be enjoyed from inside the temple rooms
✦ Sliding doors and windows frame the scenery, blending indoor and outdoor views
✦ The temple is open to the public only during spring and autumn seasons
✦ Photography is encouraged, making it a popular spot for both locals and tourists
✦ Rurikō-in provides a quiet retreat from the busy streets of Kyoto
✦ Visitors can stroll the temple grounds and enjoy the seasonal colors
✦ The temple's serene gardens and quiet halls are perfect for meditation
