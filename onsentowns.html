Kusatsu Onsen

Kusatsu Onsen: Healing Waters

★ One of Japan’s most famous hot spring towns
★ Known for high sulfur content and strong healing powers
★ Iconic Yubatake (hot water field) at the town center
★ Steamy streets lined with traditional ryokan inns
★ Rich history dating back centuries
★ Bathing culture deeply tied to local life
★ Footbaths scattered throughout the town
★ Energetic yumomi performance (water-stirring dance)
★ Surrounded by mountains and nature trails
★ Delicious regional dishes like onsen manju (steamed buns)
★ Popular year-round with snow adding charm in winter
★ Strong smell of sulfur gives authentic atmosphere
★ True symbol of Japan’s onsen heritage

Beppu Onsen

Beppu Onsen: City of Steam

★ Located in Kyushu with countless hot spring sources
★ Famous for its “Hells of Beppu” (Jigoku hot springs)
★ Steam vents rising throughout the city
★ Unique sand baths where you’re buried in warm sand
★ Mud baths for skin rejuvenation
★ Wide range of public baths and private onsens
★ Strong geothermal activity shapes the town
★ Local cuisine steamed in onsen vapors
★ Vibrant mix of traditional and modern spa resorts
★ Bustling streets with souvenir shops and eateries
★ Panoramic views of mountains and sea
★ Welcomes both locals and international visitors
★ Energetic onsen city unlike any other in Japan

Noboribetsu Onsen

Noboribetsu Onsen: to Hell Valley

★ Hokkaido’s most famous onsen town
★ Known for volcanic Jigokudani (Hell Valley)
★ Dramatic steam vents and bubbling pools
★ Water rich in minerals with healing qualities
★ Resorts and ryokan offering outdoor baths
★ Scenic walking paths through the volcanic landscape
★ Famous demon statues linked to local folklore
★ Strong sulfur smell adds mystique
★ Local specialty: Hell Valley ramen
★ Winter snow makes outdoor baths magical
★ Blend of raw nature and relaxation
★ Popular with families and couples alike
★ True symbol of Hokkaido’s volcanic power

Ibusuki Onsen

Ibusuki Onsen: Sands of Wellness

★ Located in southern Kyushu
★ Famous for natural sand baths by the beach
★ Guests buried in warm volcanic sand
★ Gentle waves create a soothing background
★ Healing effect for muscles and circulation
★ Long tradition of sand bathing in Japan
★ Coastal town with scenic ocean views
★ Combination of seaside and hot spring charm
★ Fresh local seafood served at inns
★ Strong connection to nature and sea
★ Visitors wear yukata and relax like locals
★ Peaceful atmosphere away from city life
★ Unique experience found only in Ibusuki

Gero Onsen

Gero Onsen: Japan’s Hidden Treasure

★ Ranked among Japan’s top three onsen towns
★ Located in Gifu Prefecture
★ Clear, smooth waters famous for skin benefits
★ Town filled with traditional ryokan inns
★ Free public footbaths scattered around
★ Local onsen pass lets you try multiple baths
★ River runs through the heart of town
★ Beautiful night illuminations near the bridges
★ Famous for onsen tofu and local cuisine
★ Calm, relaxed small-town atmosphere
★ Seasonal beauty in cherry blossoms and autumn leaves
★ Easy to explore on foot
★ Perfect balance of tradition and comfort

Kinosaki Onsen

Kinosaki Onsen: Town of Seven Baths

★ Charming onsen town in Hyogo Prefecture
★ Famous for seven unique public bathhouses
★ Visitors stroll the town in yukata and wooden geta
★ Canal lined with willow trees adds beauty
★ Each bathhouse has different features
★ Tradition of onsen-hopping (sotoyu-meguri)
★ Strong cultural atmosphere preserved for centuries
★ Ryokan inns serve fresh crab and seasonal dishes
★ Small-town charm with lantern-lit streets
★ Popular with couples for romantic stays
★ Cherry blossoms in spring are magical
★ Winter snows add cozy charm to baths
★ Captures the essence of Japan’s onsen spirit
