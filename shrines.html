Fushimi Inari

Kyoto

Fushimi Inari: Shrine of Endless Torii

✦ Founded in 711 AD, Kyoto's Shinto shrine dedicated to Inari, God of rice
✦ Located in southern Kyoto, at the base of Mount Inari
✦ Known for its thousands of vermilion torii gates forming scenic mountain trails
✦ Main entrance features the iconic Romon Gate welcoming visitors
✦ Stone and wooden fox statues scattered throughout, seen as Inari's messengers
✦ Offerings include rice, sake, and miniature fox figurines
✦ The hiking trail up Mount Inari takes around 2-3 hours with resting points
✦ Hatsuuma Festival in February honors Inari with rituals and ceremonies
✦ Spiritual atmosphere blends peace with the energy of countless visitors
✦ Best visited early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and enjoy light
✦ Small shrines and prayer spots hidden along the mountain path for blessings
✦ Explore nearby Fushimi sake district for traditional breweries and local shops

Hie Shrine

Takayama

Hie Shrine: Mountain Sanctuary

✦ Established in the 16th century, dedicated to the guardian deity of Takayama
✦ Located in Takayama city, Gifu Prefecture
✦ Known as the host shrine of the famous Takayama Festival
✦ The festival is held in spring and autumn with ornate floats (yatai)
✦ Floats feature intricate carvings, puppets, and traditional performances
✦ Shrine grounds are peaceful, surrounded by cedar and pine trees
✦ Main hall displays elegant Hida craftsmanship
✦ Believed to protect the city and its residents from misfortune
✦ The torii gate marks the entrance with a solemn presence
✦ Seasonal visits offer views of cherry blossoms and autumn colors
✦ Visitors pray for protection, good harvests, and safe families
✦ Historical connection to Takayama's prosperity as a castle town
✦ Explore nearby Takayama Old Town with traditional streets and sake breweries

Yamamiya Sengen

Fujinomiya

Yamamiya Sengen: Forested Fuji Retreat

✦ Founded in the Heian period as an ancient center of Mount Fuji worship
✦ Located in Fujinomiya, Shizuoka Prefecture, at the foot of Mount Fuji
✦ Dedicated to Konohanasakuya-hime, the blossom princess goddess of Fuji
✦ Served as an early starting point for pilgrims climbing Mount Fuji
✦ Unique for having no main hall, with the mountain itself as the deity
✦ Open-air altar faces Mount Fuji, creating a direct spiritual connection
✦ Sacred atmosphere with simplicity and purity preserved for centuries
✦ Annual Yamamiya Festival in November honors the goddess with rituals
✦ Important member of the Fuji Sengen shrine network across the region
✦ Closely linked with Fujisan Hongu Sengen Taisha as its auxiliary shrine
✦ Surrounded by quiet forests and natural scenery offering deep peace
✦ Explore nearby Fujinomiya city for culture, food, and clear Fuji views

Yahiko Shrine

Yahiko

Yahiko Shrine: Mountain Guardian

✦ With over 1,300 years of history, it has long been central to Niigata's spiritual life
✦ Located in Yahiko village at the base of Mount Yahiko in Niigata Prefecture
✦ Dedicated to the god Ame-no-Kagoyama-no-Mikoto, protector of the region
✦ Revered as a guardian of agriculture, fishing, and local prosperity
✦ The grand wooden torii gate marks the entrance to the shrine grounds
✦ Main hall built in elegant Shinto style, blending with the mountain backdrop
✦ Surrounding forest adds a sacred and tranquil atmosphere
✦ Visitors pray for success, safe travels, and family protection
✦ Yahiko Shrine Festival in November features traditional rituals and parades
✦ Ropeway nearby leads up Mount Yahiko for sweeping coastal views
✦ Seasonal beauty includes cherry blossoms in spring and maple leaves in autumn
✦ Long pilgrimage tradition connects the shrine with Niigata's cultural identity
✦ Explore Yahiko village with hot springs, local cuisine, and scenic walking paths

Ichimiya Asama

Ichikawamisato

Ichimiya Asama: Mount Fuji Sanctuary

✦ Founded in 865 AD, it is the chief Sengen Shrine of Kai Province
✦ Located in Fuefuki City, Yamanashi Prefecture, near the foothills of Mount Fuji
✦ Dedicated to Konohanasakuya-hime, the goddess of Mount Fuji and blossoms
✦ Holds the title of “Ichinomiya,” the highest-ranking shrine of the region
✦ Strongly connected to the ancient traditions of Fuji worship
✦ Main hall reconstructed in the Edo period with classic shrine design
✦ The grounds are surrounded by cedar trees creating a sacred atmosphere
✦ Visitors pray here for safe childbirth, fertility, and family protection
✦ Annual festivals feature Shinto rituals and traditional community events
✦ Seasonal beauty includes cherry blossoms in spring and maple leaves in autumn
✦ Historical role in guiding faith, agriculture, and local prosperity
✦ Explore nearby Fuefuki hot springs and Koshu wine country after visiting

Kifune Shrine

Kyoto

Kifune Shrine: River God Sanctuary

✦ Founded in the 5th century, it is one of Kyoto's oldest Shinto shrines
✦ Located in the mountain village of Kibune, north of Kyoto city
✦ Dedicated to Takaokami-no-kami, the deity of water and rain
✦ Known for its lantern-lined stone staircase leading to the main hall
✦ Important site for prayers related to water, rain, and agriculture
✦ Also famous for enmusubi (love and matchmaking) blessings
✦ Unique omikuji (fortune papers) reveal words only when placed in water
✦ Main hall rebuilt in 1600s, showcasing traditional Shinto architecture
✦ Seasonal beauty includes lush greenery, flowing rivers, and autumn colors
✦ Special night illuminations highlight the lantern steps in summer
✦ Visitors enjoy cool river dining experiences known as kawadoko in summer
✦ Explore nearby Kurama-dera Temple and the scenic Kibune valley trails
