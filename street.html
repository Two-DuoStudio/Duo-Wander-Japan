Japan Street

Where Every Stall Holds a Tradition

Nishiki Market

Kyoto

Nishiki Market: Kyoto’s Kitchen

★ Known as “Kyoto’s Kitchen,” stretching over 400 meters
★ Narrow street packed with more than 100 stalls and shops
★ Offers a wide variety of traditional Kyoto delicacies
★ Famous for pickles, sweets, and local snacks
★ Fresh seafood and seasonal vegetables on display
★ Long history dating back to the 14th century
★ Many vendors have been family-run for generations
★ Street buzzing with both locals and tourists
★ Chance to sample free tastings at several stalls
★ Unique souvenirs like handmade chopsticks and knives
★ Street food favorites like yakitori and mochi available
★ Covered arcade keeps the market open in all weather
★ A must-visit spot to experience Kyoto’s food culture

Kuromon Ichiba Market

Osaka

Kuromon Ichiba: Heart of Street Food

★ Nicknamed “Osaka’s Kitchen” with over 150 shops
★ Bustling atmosphere with fresh seafood as the star
★ Famous for tuna, crab, and grilled scallops
★ Street stalls selling ready-to-eat skewers and snacks
★ Popular among chefs buying ingredients for restaurants
★ Open since the early 1900s with deep history
★ Mix of raw ingredients and cooked street food
★ Local Osaka specialties like takoyaki and okonomiyaki
★ Vendors are lively and love interacting with customers
★ Clean and organized despite the large crowds
★ Fresh fruit stalls offering beautifully cut portions
★ Atmosphere reflects Osaka’s “kuidaore” (eat till you drop) culture
★ Great place to feel the city’s food-loving spirit

Ameya-Yokocho

Tokyo

Ameya-Yokocho: Market to Bazaar

★ Located just outside Ueno Station, Tokyo
★ Originally a post-war black market in the 1940s
★ Today, it’s a lively shopping and food street
★ Stalls selling dried foods, spices, and sweets
★ Affordable street food like takoyaki and kebabs
★ Bustling crowds moving through narrow streets
★ Mix of Japanese and international foods available
★ Clothing, cosmetics, and discount goods also sold
★ Energy of the place feels chaotic but exciting
★ Vendors shouting to attract customers
★ Seasonal decorations add to the lively mood
★ Nighttime visits give a more local feel
★ One of Tokyo’s best places for bargain-hunting

Omicho Market

Kanazawa

Omicho Market: Seafood of the North

★ Central market in Kanazawa for over 280 years
★ Famous for fresh seafood from the Sea of Japan
★ Known especially for crab, yellowtail, and shrimp
★ Offers sushi stalls with ultra-fresh ingredients
★ Narrow streets lined with covered stalls
★ Strong local vibe with Kanazawa specialties
★ Mix of street food and sit-down eateries
★ Seasonal produce and traditional sweets also found
★ Market is always buzzing with local shoppers
★ Vendors happily explain their products to tourists
★ Small restaurants serve bowls of kaisendon (seafood rice)
★ Atmosphere combines tradition with daily life
★ A perfect stop to explore Kanazawa’s food culture

Makishi Public Market

Okinawa

Makishi Public Market: Island Flavors

★ Known as the “Kitchen of Okinawa” for decades
★ Market reflects Okinawa’s unique food culture
★ Colorful stalls selling pork, seafood, and tropical fruits
★ Exotic ingredients like sea grapes and bitter melon
★ Strong Chinese and Southeast Asian influence in flavors
★ Locals shopping alongside curious visitors
★ 2nd floor food court where vendors cook what you buy
★ Street buzzing with Okinawan dishes like taco rice
★ Vendors are friendly and love explaining foods
★ Lively and colorful atmosphere unlike mainland markets
★ Popular stop for both locals and tourists
★ Surrounding arcades add to the shopping fun
★ Truly captures Okinawa’s island spirit and culture

Hakodate Morning Market

Hokkaido

Hakodate Morning: Bounty at Dawn

★ Located right next to Hakodate Station
★ Famous for its fresh seafood breakfast stalls
★ Specialty dish is the kaisendon seafood rice bowl
★ Tourists love the squid fishing tank where you catch your meal
★ Stalls selling fresh crab, scallops, and sea urchin
★ Vibrant and colorful displays of seafood and produce
★ Local farmers bring fresh vegetables and melons
★ Early morning buzz with locals and tourists mixed
★ Vendors prepare food on the spot for customers
★ Known for friendly service with warm Hokkaido spirit
★ Covered areas make it easy to walk in any weather
★ Market feels authentic and community-driven
★ Perfect place to start the day in Hakodate
